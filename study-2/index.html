The thesis

The money is in taste, access and trust — not the AI.

AI is trusted to plan but not to book. A thin AI wrapper has no moat; defensibility is structural — proprietary supply, clean data, liability rails. At the luxury end, curation and judgment are worth most, and AI augments the human rather than replacing them. The opportunity is to be the brand a discerning traveller chooses for its point of view.

The white space

The corner no one owns at scale.

Incumbents own access but speak in identical "luxury/bespoke" language. AI-natives own technology but lack relationships and stall at the trust gate. The bionic host is taste-neutral. No one combines a distinctive editorial voice, human delivery, owned supply, and AI as leverage.

The model

The mix shifts from transactional to recurring.

Commission alone is a sub-5%-margin commodity. Fees lift net margin to 12–20% and signal quality. Membership banks recurring, drag-free value once trust is earned. The cost structure is people, not technology — AI is leased to raise curator capacity.

Verdict · Conditional GO

Build it — as a brand, not a wrapper.

There is a defensible, fundable luxury agency to build — The Editorial House, entered through a vertical wedge and matured into membership. It clears the gate on all three lenses; the live risk is whether the affluent tier pays for taste and membership as distinct from access and time.
