Central thesis

AI is moving travel's adjacent profit pools from production to legibility and judgment.

Production — content volume, tier-1 servicing, SEO-era discoverability — is being cost-out by AI. What's being created is a new, budgeted set of problems: being visible to AI engines, legible to AI agents, and governed well enough to be trusted with both. Consumer-facing adjacencies stay closed behind licences, payment flows and distribution. The durable small-operator opportunity is B2B, judgment-priced, and at the edges.

VERDICT · GO

GO — on the B2B legibility stack: an AI-visibility practice for travel brands (Z1), paired with travel-specialised AI governance advisory (Z3), distributed through an owned intelligence wedge (Z7), with agentic-readiness (Z2) and the productised benchmark (Z4) gated behind explicit triggers.

Confidence: moderate-high on direction, moderate on magnitude. Anchor desirability and feasibility evidence is strong and multi-sourced; viability rests partly on young, aggregator-grade market forecasts — hence the falsifiers below. Independently stress-tested in Stage 6; see the red-team review.

FALSIFIER 1If AI-engine citation share proves unmovable by earned-media and structured-authority work within 2–3 client quarters, Z1's retainer logic collapses to one-off audits.
FALSIFIER 2If platforms make AI visibility a paid placement (ads inside answers), the organic GEO services pool shrinks toward media buying.
FALSIFIER 3If the Q2 2026 agentic pipelines fail to shift measurable booking volume by mid-2027, Z2 stays gated and the agentic-urgency narrative weakens.
FALSIFIER 4If Big-4 and GDS professional-services arms productise travel AI governance and readiness by 2027, Z2/Z3 pricing compresses; the vertical wedge must already be established.
