Central thesis

The durable margin around travel sits with whoever holds the accountability.

AI is cost-out for production — paperwork, content, tier-one servicing. What it cannot cheapen is answering for a regulated, cross-border act. Where AI only advises, value leaks to the model. Where someone must act and carry the liability — a visa, a permanent-establishment exposure, a compliance audit — the efficiency lands as margin, not price. Consumer-facing edges stay closed behind licences, devaluation and incumbents. The durable opening is B2B, act-side, and priced on accountability.

VERDICT · CONDITIONAL GO

GO — on owning the regulated cross-border mobility-compliance layer: the paperwork-with-accountability band where AI compresses cost but the operator keeps the margin, sold as annuity SaaS plus value-added services, riding the enforcement wave.

Against the standing options: it complements Study 1's supply-side zones (a peer pool with a more durable, structural moat) and dominates Study 2's Editorial House on size and defensibility. P(thesis correct at 12 months) = 57%. Confidence: moderate-high on direction — two independent research passes converged on the accountability layer — moderate on the wedge and on aggregator-grade magnitudes. Independently red-teamed; see the review.

FALSIFIER 1If Deel-class EOR incumbents extend from payroll into PE-risk and immigration compliance, the wedge closes before a new entrant scales — the central risk.
FALSIFIER 2If the compliance work commoditises to checklist tasks, AI's saving competes down to price instead of becoming margin — the thesis only holds where liability is concentrated.
FALSIFIER 3If regulators mandate issuer-side or "compliance-by-default" automation, the paperwork intermediary is disintermediated — the same pattern now threatening claims recovery.
FALSIFIER 4If the sovereign-contract ceiling and the EOR floor leave no ownable band between them, the anchor collapses to a feature inside someone else's platform.
